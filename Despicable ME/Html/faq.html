Frequently Asked Questions



About the Movies

What is the chronological order of the Despicable Me movies?
The Despicable Me movies should be watched in this order: Despicable Me (2010), Despicable Me 2 (2013), Minions (2015), Despicable Me 3 (2017), Minions: The Rise of Gru (2022), and Despicable Me 4 (2024).

Who are the main characters in the Despicable Me series?
The main characters include Gru, voiced by Steve Carell, his adopted daughters Margo, Edith, and Agnes, the Minions (Kevin, Bob, and Stuart), Lucy Wilde, and Dr. Nefario.

What is the main plot of the Despicable Me series?
The series follows Gru, a supervillain who adopts three orphaned girls as part of a plan to steal the moon. Over time, he grows to love the girls and transforms from a villain into a devoted father.


Streaming and Merchandise

Where can I stream or purchase the Despicable Me movies?
The Despicable Me movies are available on various streaming platforms such as Netflix, Amazon Prime Video, and Hulu. They can also be purchased on digital stores like iTunes, Google Play, and Amazon.

Are there any video games or merchandise based on the Despicable Me series?
Yes, there are several video games, toys, clothing, and other merchandise based on the Despicable Me series. Popular games include Despicable Me: Minion Rush and various mobile apps. You can find official merchandise at major retailers and online stores.


Characters and Minions

Who voices the character of Gru in the Despicable Me series?
The character of Gru is voiced by Steve Carell, who brings a unique and memorable performance to the role.

How many Minions are there, and do they have names?
There are hundreds of Minions, each with their own unique personalities. Some of the main Minions with names include Kevin, Bob, Stuart, Dave, and Jerry. These characters often take on significant roles in the movies.

What makes the Minions so popular?
The Minions are beloved for their playful, mischievous nature, their unique language (Minionese), and their hilarious, slapstick humor. Their childlike innocence and loyalty to Gru also endear them to audiences of all ages.
